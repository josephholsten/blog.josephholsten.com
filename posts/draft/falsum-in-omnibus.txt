There must be some pleasure in running away, in avoiding a gaze, in tiptoeing around. It hurts so much when the people we care about avoid us, there must be some pleasure in it. The masochism of willful neglect isn't the monopoly of alcoholics and codependents. But it must be easier to say, “it was the substances, not sufficiently controlled. With them going out of control, how could I but follow their lead?” Nor unique to crazies, eyes wide to other worlds demanding attention, or driven dancing ahead in manic passion, or chained in continual search of just so.
